THE CO2 OF AIRGUNS: TIPS AND TRICKS

CO2 is a convenient and easy-to-use power source in many air guns to launch a pellet or BB downrange. It comes in different sizes and formats but your most common will be 12 gram and 88 gram cartridges.

CO2 is readily available and easy to carry. When using CO2, use a drop of silicone oil on the tip of each new capsule to help maintain the integrity of your air gun seals. This will keep the seals and internals lubricated for a longer air gun life.

CO2 is a gas, so the lower the outside temperature when using CO2, the lower the gas pressure, and therefore lower shooting velocities. Temperatures below 65 degrees Fahrenheit can also freeze the airgun value. On cold days, keep your CO2 in a warm location. A couple of hot hands and a jacket pocket or small ice chest are a great way to keep your CO2 to temperature and in working order. Just be sure not to put your CO2 on a direct heat source like a wood stove.
